Firearms has been a lifelong interest for AJ. Naturally it reached an apex point in the Army Ordnance Corps. Since AJ pursues his hobbies to the nth detail his interest expanded into hand loading, USPSA target shooting events, as well as various precision rifle contests and matches.

AJ has always been an advocate for safety. He not only talks the talk but he took action. AJ eventually obtained and still holds certifications in pistol, rifle, and as a range safety officer.

AJ believes that shooting is a perishable skill, a skill that must be practiced on a routine basis. In addition to practice, he believes that a good shooter must push to become constantly better. That las point is something AJ does with his golfing and cooking skills

The Five Laws of Gun Safety

  1. Always treat every gun as if it were loaded.
  2. Always keep the gun pointed in a safe direction.
  3. Never point your gun at anything you do not intend to shoot.
  4. Always keep your finger off the trigger until you are ready to shoot.
  5. Always be aware of your target and what lies beyond it.
